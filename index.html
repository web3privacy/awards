Web3Privacy Now Awards

Annual community driven awards given to the best and worst within the privacy ecosystem

Announcement: 30 December 2024 during livestream

What are the Privacy Awards?

The state of privacy within web3 changes every year. New research papers, projects, demos, and events are delivered. We want to give the privacy community a voice & celebrate annual privacy contributions worldwide.

We ask leaders behind privacy protocols, enthusiasts, developers, researchers, and community builders to share their opinions on the year's crucial privacy projects, people, and events.

Our goals help privacy projects get a new audience facilitate community contribution to privacy knowledge sharing create a discussion around the annual privacy footprint.

The Awards Process

We use the following methodology during the nominations, voting and awards process

Nomination process: Any person can Nominate a candidate for any of the 4 categories of Awards this year, by answering the following questions:

1 - What is your favourite pro-privacy project/product of 2024? *Name one*

2 - What was the major news or key event regarding privacy in 2024? *Name two*

3 - What is the most exciting innovation (new research, protocols, or approaches) supporting privacy? *Name two*

4 - Doxxer of the year (any project, person, or organisation that sought to reduce privacy within the space). *Name one*

NOMINATE YOUR CHOICES FOR 2024 PRIVACY AWARDS
It is very easy to submit your nominations and your choices matter to the Awards - any person can submit a nomination (no vetting)

Write out your nominations by answering each of the 4 questions

Submit your nominations to the team via one of the following:

+ Send a DM via X / Twitter to the web3privacy profile

+ Join our Matrix Group Chat and share it there with our community

+ Send us an email to web3privacynow@protonmail.com

Note: if you are affiliated with a privacy project (work or invest in them) please name a different one, this will reduce the bias in our nominations and make the Awards better overall. We respect anonymity and privacy, we welcome submissions from all users including from anon/pseudonymous ones.
